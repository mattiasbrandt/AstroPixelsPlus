What is Easing? Easing controls how the servo accelerates and decelerates. Real objects don't instantly reach full speed—easing makes motion look natural.
Quick Guide: Ease In = starts slow, speeds up. Ease Out = starts fast, slows down. In/Out = both. Higher numbers = more pronounced effect.
Recommended: Use 3 — Ease In/Out Quad for most panel movements. It provides natural motion without being too slow.

Best Practice Panel Adjustment
MG90S Servo Range: This slider controls the PWM pulse width sent to the servo. The safe operating range is 800–2200 μs (center = 1500 μs). Values outside this range may cause servo buzzing or mechanical stress.
Calibration Steps:
1) Select a panel from the dropdown above.
2) Drag the slider to find the ideal open and closed positions. Note: Each servo has slight manufacturing differences—what works for Panel 1 may not be perfect for Panel 2.
3) Click Move (:MV) to test the position temporarily without saving.
4) When satisfied with the open position, click Save Open (#SO).
5) Repeat for the closed position, then click Save Closed (#SC).
Tips: If a servo buzzes at a position, move the slider slightly away from the extreme. Most panels work well with values around 900 μs (closed) and 2100 μs (open).
Quick swap shortcut: #SWxx swaps open/closed values instantly. Use only during calibration, never at boot.
